Project Overview

We developed an advanced machine learning system that analyzes text to identify potential indicators of suicidal ideation and depression. Our system uses Natural Language Processing (NLP) and applied TF-IDF vectorization to classify text into two categories: suicide-related and non-suicide-related content, achieving high accuracy through rigorous training and validation.

Our Methodology

Text Normalization Process

We implemented a comprehensive text preprocessing pipeline to clean and standardize the data:

1 Data Cleaning

Removed missing values, duplicate entries, and special characters. Converted all text to lowercase and standardized formatting for consistency.

2 Tokenization

Split text into individual words (tokens) using NLTK's word tokenizer. Average of 130 tokens per text before processing.

3 Stop Words Removal

Eliminated common words (e.g., "the", "is", "at") that don't contribute meaningful information to mental health assessment.

4 Text Normalization

Filtered tokens with length > 2 characters. Reduced average tokens from 130 to 61, focusing on psychologically significant words.

5 Feature Extraction (TF-IDF)

Applied Term Frequency-Inverse Document Frequency (TF-IDF) vectorization with 5,000 features, bigrams, and frequency filtering (min_df=2, max_df=0.8) to convert text into numerical representations.

Selected Model: Support Vector Machine (SVM)

We selected SVM as our final model due to its exceptional accuracy of 93.15%. SVM excels at text classification by finding the optimal decision boundary (hyperplane) that maximally separates suicide-related and non-suicide-related content in high-dimensional feature space. The kernel proved most effective for our TF-IDF features, providing both high accuracy and computational efficiency.

Understanding the Metrics
  • Accuracy (93.15%): Overall correctness of predictions across all samples
  • Precision: When we predict a class, how often are we correct?
  • Recall: Of all actual instances of a class, how many did we correctly identify?
  • F1-Score: Harmonic mean balancing precision and recall (93.09% average)
  • Balanced Performance: Model performs equally well on both suicide and non-suicide classes

How It Works

Behind the Scenes
  1. Text Input: User enters text for analysis through the web interface.
  2. Preprocessing: Text is cleaned, tokenized, and stop words are removed.
  3. Feature Extraction: TF-IDF vectorization converts text into numerical features (5,000-dimensional vector).
  4. Classification: SVM model analyzes the feature vector and predicts the class.
  5. Risk Assessment: Based on prediction and confidence, a risk level (low/moderate/high) is assigned.
  6. Results Display: Prediction, confidence score, and appropriate resources are shown to the user.

Limitations & Important Notes

Understanding the Limitations
  • Not a Diagnostic Tool: This system cannot diagnose mental health conditions and should never be used for that purpose.
  • Text-Only Analysis: Mental health assessment requires comprehensive evaluation including tone, context, history, and professional judgment.
  • False Positives/Negatives: Despite 93.15% accuracy, the model can make mistakes. Some non-suicide content may be flagged, and some suicide-related content may be missed.
  • Cultural and Linguistic Limitations: Model trained primarily on English text and may not capture cultural nuances in expression.
  • Context Matters: The system analyzes text patterns without understanding full context, relationships, or individual circumstances.
  • No Substitute for Professional Help: Always consult qualified mental health professionals for assessment and treatment.
